Breaking the glass

Near the close of a Jewish wedding, the groom breaks a small glass, reminding us that even in the happiest of times we must not forget our responsibility of Tikkun Olam (repairing brokenness in the world).

Chuppah

The chuppah is the canopy under which Sara and Frank will stand during the wedding ceremony. It represents the new home they will build together. This chuppah was made by Sara's parents and is decorated with tallitot from Charles Keil and Irwin Fridovich (Sara's grandfathers), Isadore Silverman (Sara's great-grandfather), and a shawl patterned after a traditional Chinese design on display at the National Palace Museum in Taipei, Taiwan.

Hakafot

During the hakafot ("circling"), Sara and Frank will circle each other seven times (3 each + 1 together). Seven represents the days of creation, and marriage is an act of creation as well.

Hora

The hora is an Ashkenazi Jewish circle dance of celebration traditionally accompanied by klezmer music. During the hora, it is customary for the bride and groom to be lifted on chairs within the circle, as a symbol of the newlyweds' elevation and joy.

Ketubah

The ketubah is a traditional Jewish marriage contract. Sara and Frank's ketubah (on display inside the house) was signed by them, 2 witnesses, and Rabbi Ira Dounn before the wedding ceremony.

Kiddush

The Kiddush is a Jewish blessing and prayer recited over wine or grape juice to sanctify the Sabbath, Jewish holidays, and important milestones in life. The kiddush cup used in the ceremony originally belonged to Max Keil, Sara's great-great-grandfather.

Kiddushin

In Jewish law, kiddushin ("betrothal") is the stage of a Jewish wedding in which the bride and groom mutually promise to marry each other by exchanging rings and reciting in Hebrew: "You are holy to me as my husband/wife, in accordance with the tradition of Moses and Israel."

Kippot

Kippot (singular kippah) are brimless caps worn on the head as a sign of respect for G-d by all in attendance who wish to wear one.

Ring Design

The ring design on the cover of this program includes Sara's and Frank's Hebrew names (Zahava Chaya and Gil) connected by modified lemniscate (infinity) symbols.

Tallit

Tallitot (plural of “tallit”) are traditional Jewish prayer shawls.

Yichud

The yichud is the traditional period of seclusion during which the newlyweds spend their first few minutes of marriage alone together, immediately after the ceremony. After the yichud, Sara and Frank will rejoin the celebration!
